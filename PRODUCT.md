# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Store staff (cashiers and managers) at BRON, a B2B ferretería/materiales de construcción. Context: standing at a POS counter under time pressure, processing 100–150 transactions per day. Primary task: register a sale quickly, accurately, and without error. Users are comfortable with computers but not tech specialists; they need a tool that disappears into the task.

## Product Purpose

Replace the closed external POS system (Professional Plus) with a fully in-house system. Eliminate double-entry across modules, enable real-time client portals and accounts, and preserve AFIP e-invoicing integration. Success means a cashier can complete a full sale cycle — search product, set client, confirm payment, issue AFIP invoice — in under 30 seconds, with zero data loss.

## Brand Personality

Sólido, rápido, confiable. BRON is a real business with its own identity, not generic SaaS. Tone: direct, undecorated, trustworthy. Confidence is earned through reliability and clarity, not visual flair.

## Anti-references

- **Excel glorificado**: everything in flat tables, no visual hierarchy, no identity, no affordances — looks like someone threw data into a grid and stopped.
- **Caja registradora vieja**: oversized fonts, terminal/retro aesthetics, clunky affordances that feel dated rather than solid.

## Design Principles

1. **Speed above all** — every interaction must minimize friction. The cashier's hands stay on the keyboard; the mouse is secondary.
2. **Keyboard-first flows** — every primary workflow (search, add product, confirm sale, navigate screens) must be completable without touching the mouse.
3. **Zero ambiguity** — labels, states, and feedback must be unambiguous. A cashier making an error under pressure costs the business money.
4. **Owned identity** — the system should look like BRON's system, not off-the-shelf SaaS. The brand color (dark red/bordeaux) and identity should be present and confident, not decorative.
5. **Trust through reliability** — the visual language must signal that this tool won't fail. No superfluous animation, no decorative uncertainty, no cute interactions that slow things down.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Keyboard navigation is critical — cashiers rarely lift hands from keyboard. WCAG AA contrast minimum (4.5:1 body text). Old PC hardware means no animation-heavy CSS or JavaScript-intensive effects. No reduced-motion exemptions needed if animations are minimal by default.
